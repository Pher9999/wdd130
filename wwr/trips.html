Tubing Adventures
  • Close-Up Exploration: The raft's shallow draft allows it to get extremely close to shorelines, hidden coves, volcanic sea caves, and towering waterfalls that larger cruise ships cannot reach.
  • Wildlife Sightings: These trips are prime for spotting marine and coastal wildlife, including humpback whales, orcas, sea otters, seals, bald eagles, and sometimes bears or mountain goats.
  • Guided Insight: A naturalist or knowledgeable captain typically provides a narrative on local history, geology, and the importance of the waterways to indigenous peoples.
  • Participation Requirements:
    Age/Height: Usually a minimum age of 10 years and a minimum height of 4 feet.
    Physicality: Participants must be able to step in and out of the raft. The ride is bumpy and jarring, so it is generally not recommended for those with back, neck, or joint issues, or those who are pregnant.
  • The Thrill of the Rapids: You will work as a team under the guidance of a professional to navigate various "Classes" of water. These range from Class I (calm, fast-moving water) to Class V (technical, powerful rapids).
  • Immersive Nature: Trips often pass through deep canyons and diverse ecosystems inaccessible by land. You might see towering sandstone cliffs, ancient petroglyphs, and wildlife like bald eagles, bighorn sheep, or river otters.
  • Half-Day/Full-Day Trips: Ideal for beginners or those short on time. These often include a few hours of paddling, safety briefings, and sometimes a riverside BBQ lunch.
  • Multi-Day Expeditions: For a deeper escape, multi-day trips (3–15 days) involve camping on sandy beaches under the stars, gourmet riverside meals, and hiking to hidden waterfalls.
  • The Float: Most trips last between 90 minutes and 4 hours, depending on the river’s flow and your selected route.
  • Difficulty: Standard tubing occurs on Class I "lazy rivers" with calm water and occasional gentle ripples.
  • Safety & Swimming: Because there is no guide in your tube, the ability to swim is often mandatory as there is a high probability of entering the water during the run.
  • Self-Guided Freedom: You can often "captain your own boat," allowing you to take on the rapids at your own pace while enjoying scenic river views.
  • Activities: Beyond floating, many trips include designated spots for swimming, rock jumping, or stopping on river islands for a picnic.
